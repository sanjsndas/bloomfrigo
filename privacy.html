Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2023

1. Information We Collect

Bloomfigo collects personal information when you register for our services, make purchases, or interact with our website. This includes name, email, phone number, and payment details. We also gather non-personal data like browser type, IP address, and browsing behavior to improve user experience. Cookies help us track preferences and service usage patterns. Information may be collected during in-person consultations or therapy sessions too. We only collect data that's relevant to providing our gardening therapy services.

2. How We Use Your Information

Your personal information helps us deliver personalized gardening therapy services and improve your experience. We use it to process bookings, send service updates, and respond to inquiries. Collected data allows us to develop new therapeutic programs based on client needs. Information may be used for marketing with your consent, where you can opt-out anytime. Non-personal data helps analyze website performance and user behavior patterns. We never sell your data to third parties for marketing purposes.

3. Data Protection Measures

Bloomfigo implements industry-standard security protocols to protect your information. All data transmissions are encrypted using SSL technology to prevent unauthorized access. Our servers are protected by firewalls and regularly monitored for vulnerabilities. Access to personal information is restricted to authorized personnel only. We conduct regular security audits to maintain protection standards. Physical documents containing sensitive data are securely stored and shredded when no longer needed.

4. Third-Party Data Sharing

We may share necessary information with trusted service providers who assist in delivering our gardening therapy programs. This includes payment processors, booking system providers, and therapeutic service partners. All third parties must comply with strict confidentiality agreements and data protection standards. We may disclose information when required by law or to protect our legal rights. Aggregate, non-personal data may be shared for research or statistical analysis. You will be notified of any significant changes to our sharing practices.

5. Your Privacy Rights

You have the right to access, correct, or delete your personal information in our records. Requests must be made in writing and verified for security purposes. You can opt-out of marketing communications at any time via unsubscribe links. Clients may request restrictions on how their data is processed or used. We provide options to download your data for personal use or transfer to another provider. Appeals regarding privacy decisions can be submitted to our Data Protection Officer.

6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies to enhance functionality and remember user preferences. Essential cookies are necessary for core site functions like account logins. Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our content. Marketing cookies allow targeted advertising based on browsing behavior. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings. Note that disabling cookies may affect certain website features and service performance.

7. Data Retention Policy

Personal data is retained as long as necessary to fulfill service agreements and legal obligations. Therapy session records are kept for 7 years to comply with health service regulations. Inactive accounts may be archived after 3 years of inactivity. Financial records are maintained for 10 years for tax and audit purposes. When data is no longer needed, it's securely deleted or anonymized. You may request specific retention periods for your information.

8. International Data Transfers

As a global service, your data may be transferred and processed in countries outside your residence. We ensure all international transfers comply with applicable data protection laws. For EU residents, Standard Contractual Clauses are used when transferring data outside the EEA. Our cloud storage providers maintain servers in multiple countries for redundancy. Where possible, regional data centers are used to minimize cross-border transfers.

9. Children's Privacy

Our gardening therapy programs for children under 13 require parental consent for data collection. We collect minimal information necessary for safe participation in activities. Parents may review, edit, or delete their child's information upon request. Special safeguards are implemented for therapy records involving minors. Participation in some programs may require health information relevant to safety concerns. Children's data is never used for marketing purposes without explicit consent.

10. Payment Information Security

All payment transactions are processed through PCI-DSS compliant service providers. Credit card details are encrypted and never stored on our servers. Recurring payments require tokenization for enhanced security. We implement additional authentication measures for high-value transactions. Payment processors provide fraud detection and prevention services. Discrepancies or suspicious activity will prompt immediate investigation.

11. Data Breach Procedures

We maintain an incident response plan to address potential data breaches. Suspected breaches are investigated immediately by our security team. Affected individuals will be notified within 72 hours of breach confirmation when required by law. Regulatory authorities are informed of significant breaches per legal requirements. Remediation measures are implemented to prevent repeat occurrences. Post-breach reviews help strengthen security defenses.

12. Policy Changes and Updates

This privacy policy may be updated periodically to reflect changes in services or regulations. Significant changes will be communicated through email notifications or website banners. The "last updated" date at the top indicates the current version. Continued use of our services after changes implies acceptance of the updated policy. Archived versions are available upon request for comparison. Major changes affecting user rights may require re-consent.

13. Special Category Data

Therapy services may require collection of sensitive health data for treatment purposes. This information receives the highest level of protection under our systems. Access is restricted to authorized therapeutic staff only. Medical information is only collected when relevant to gardening therapy services. Special category data is never used for marketing purposes. Additional consent is obtained before collecting or processing such data.

14. Automated Decision Making

We may use algorithms to recommend personalized therapy programs based on your profile. These automated systems are regularly audited for fairness and accuracy. Significant automated decisions affecting service access can be reviewed manually. Clients may request human intervention in automated processes. Machine learning models are trained on anonymized aggregate data. Algorithmic bias assessments are conducted every 6 months.

15. Social Media Integration

Our website includes social media plugins that may collect browsing data. These third-party services operate under their own privacy policies. We don't control data collected by social media platforms. Sharing content via social buttons may transmit information to those networks. Social logins provide convenience but share profile information with us. You should review privacy settings on each social platform.

16. Email Communication

Service-related emails are sent to facilitate bookings and provide therapy updates. Marketing emails will only be sent with your explicit consent. Unsubscribe options are included in all promotional communications. Our email systems use encryption and authentication technologies. We track open rates to improve communication effectiveness. Personally identifiable data is never included in unsecured emails.

17. Website Analytics

We use Google Analytics to understand user behavior on our website. Collected data includes page views, session duration, and navigation paths. IP addresses are anonymized to protect visitor privacy. Analytics help us optimize content and service accessibility. Custom reports focus on service-related metrics rather than personal identification. You can opt-out of analytics tracking via browser extensions or settings.

18. Client Testimonials

With consent, we may publish client success stories featuring first names or initials. Detailed personal information is never included without explicit permission. Testimonials may be used across marketing materials and our website. Clients can request removal of their testimonials at any time. Quotes may be edited for clarity while maintaining original meaning. Published testimonials are reviewed annually for continued relevance.

19. Research and Development

Anonymized data may be used for horticultural therapy research purposes. Published research never identifies individual participants. Participation in research studies is always voluntary. Data used for product development is aggregated from multiple sources. Research findings help improve therapeutic gardening techniques. You may opt-out of having your data included in research.

20. Legal Compliance

We comply with all applicable privacy laws including GDPR, CCPA, and HIPAA where relevant. Client records are available for lawful government requests with proper documentation. Legal counsel reviews all compliance procedures annually. Staff receive regular training on privacy law requirements. Our policies are updated to reflect changes in legislation. A dedicated compliance officer oversees all privacy-related matters.

21. User Accounts and Profiles

Account registration requires minimal personal information for service access. Profile details can be updated at any time through your dashboard. Secure passwords and two-factor authentication options are available. Inactive accounts may be deactivated after prolonged inactivity. You can download your profile data for personal records. Account deletion requests are processed within 30 days.

22. Employee Access to Data

Only authorized personnel can access client information for legitimate purposes. Staff undergo thorough background checks before handling sensitive data. Access levels are assigned based on job responsibilities. All employees sign confidentiality agreements upon hiring. Data access is logged and monitored for suspicious activity. Terminated employees immediately lose all system access.

23. Video and Photography

We may record therapy sessions for training and quality assurance purposes. Photography during garden activities will only occur with participant consent. Images may be used in marketing materials with written permission. You can request to review and approve images before publication. Digital media is stored securely with restricted access. Consent can be withdrawn at any time for future use.

24. Data Accuracy

We take reasonable steps to ensure collected information is accurate and current. Clients are encouraged to review and update their details regularly. Discrepancies discovered by staff will be verified with you directly. Outdated records are archived according to retention policies. Annual data audits help maintain information quality. You may request corrections to any inaccurate personal data.

25. Newsletter Subscription

Our gardening therapy newsletter provides seasonal tips and program updates. Subscription requires double opt-in confirmation for security. Each email includes clear unsubscribe instructions. Newsletter analytics don't track individual recipient activity. Content can be customized based on expressed interests. Subscription preferences can be adjusted in your account settings.

26. Facility Security

Our therapy centers have physical security measures to protect client information. Paper records are stored in locked cabinets with limited access. Electronic devices are password-protected and encrypted. Security cameras monitor sensitive areas without recording private sessions. Visitors must sign in and be accompanied by staff. Facility audits verify compliance with security protocols.

27. Health Information Management

Therapeutic gardening programs may involve collecting health-related information. These records are maintained separately from other personal data. Access requires additional authentication and logging. Health data is only shared with other providers when necessary for treatment. Secure messaging systems protect electronic health communications. HIPAA compliance is maintained for US-based clients.

28. Appointment Scheduling

Our booking system collects necessary details for therapy session scheduling. Calendar integration shows availability while protecting other appointments. Reminders are sent via preferred communication channels. Cancellation information helps optimize scheduling practices. Waitlist functionality maintains your place without duplicating data entry. Booking preferences can be customized in your profile.

29. Online Community Features

Participation in discussion forums requires acceptance of community guidelines. Posted content is moderated for appropriateness and privacy concerns. Usernames can be pseudonyms to protect identity. Private messaging between members is monitored for safety. You control what profile information is visible to others. Community access can be restricted or revoked for policy violations.

30. Service Customization Data

Information about your gardening preferences helps personalize therapy sessions. Collected data may include plant preferences, physical abilities, and therapeutic goals. Custom program recommendations are generated from this information. You can adjust preferences anytime to change service offerings. Data is aggregated to improve our therapy methodologies. Participation in customization features is always optional.

31. Business Transfers

In the event of company sale or merger, client data may be transferred to new owners. You will be notified of such transfers and any changes to privacy practices. Data protection agreements will be maintained during transitions. Options for data deletion before transfer will be provided when possible. Your continued use of services implies consent to the transfer. Legal obligations regarding data handling will be honored.

32. Advertising and Retargeting

Online ads may be displayed based on your browsing behavior and preferences. We use third-party advertising networks to serve these ads. You can opt-out of personalized advertising through your browser settings or by visiting the Network Advertising Initiative website. Retargeting helps us reach users who have previously interacted with our services. We do not share personal information with advertisers without your consent. Ad performance is monitored to improve our marketing strategies.

33. Feedback and Surveys

We may conduct surveys to gather feedback on our services and improve client experiences. Participation is voluntary, and responses are anonymized for analysis. Survey results help us identify areas for improvement and client satisfaction levels. You can provide feedback through various channels, including email and our website. Feedback may be used in aggregate form for reporting purposes. We value your input and strive to implement suggestions where feasible.

34. Client Support Services

Our support team is available to assist with any inquiries regarding privacy and data protection. You can reach out via email or phone for assistance. Support interactions may be recorded for quality assurance and training purposes. We aim to respond to all inquiries promptly and thoroughly. Client satisfaction is our priority, and we welcome your questions. Support staff are trained in privacy policies and procedures.

35. Data Minimization Principle

We adhere to the principle of data minimization, collecting only the information necessary for service delivery. Unnecessary data collection is avoided to protect your privacy. Regular reviews ensure that we do not retain excessive information. You can request the deletion of any data that is no longer relevant. Our goal is to maintain a balance between service quality and privacy. Data minimization helps reduce risks associated with data breaches.

36. Client Education Resources

We provide educational materials on privacy rights and data protection. Resources are available on our website and during therapy sessions. Clients are encouraged to understand their rights regarding personal information. Workshops may be offered to discuss privacy topics in-depth. We aim to empower clients with knowledge about their data. Educational resources are updated regularly to reflect current laws and practices.

37. Data Processing Agreements

We have data processing agreements in place with all third-party service providers. These agreements outline responsibilities regarding data protection and compliance. Regular audits ensure that partners adhere to our privacy standards. We only work with vendors who demonstrate a commitment to data security. Any changes to processing agreements will be communicated to clients. You can request copies of these agreements for review.

38. Client Rights Under GDPR

For clients in the EU, we comply with GDPR regulations regarding personal data processing. You have the right to access, rectify, and erase your data. The right to data portability allows you to transfer your information to another service. You can object to processing or request restrictions under certain conditions. We provide clear instructions on how to exercise these rights. Our Data Protection Officer is available for assistance with GDPR inquiries.

39. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach, we will notify affected individuals promptly. Notifications will include details about the breach and potential impacts. We will provide guidance on steps to mitigate risks. Regulatory authorities will be informed as required by law. Our incident response plan ensures swift action to address breaches. We continuously improve our security measures to prevent future incidents.
