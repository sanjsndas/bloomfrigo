Cookie Policy

Last updated: June 2023

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit websites. They help websites remember information about your visit and preferences. Cookies enable core functionality like page navigation and access to secure areas. They also help website owners understand how visitors interact with their site. Some cookies are essential for technical reasons, while others enhance user experience. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify settings to decline them if preferred. Different types of cookies serve various purposes from security to analytics.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

Bloomfigo uses several types of cookies to enhance your experience on our site. Strictly necessary cookies enable core functionality and security features. Preference cookies remember your choices like language settings. Analytical cookies help us understand visitor behavior and improve our site. Marketing cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements both on and off our site. Some cookies are session-based and expire when you close your browser, while others persist for longer periods. We periodically review our cookie usage to ensure we're respecting your privacy while providing good service.

3. Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are critical for the basic functionality of our website and cannot be disabled. These include cookies that maintain your login session and remember items in your shopping cart. Security cookies help detect malicious activity and protect user data. Load balancer cookies distribute traffic across servers to optimize performance. Some essential cookies help ensure accessibility features work properly. If you block these cookies, certain parts of our site may not function as intended. We use the minimum necessary essential cookies to provide our services.

4. Performance Cookies

Performance cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, which pages are most popular, and error messages. This helps us improve how our website works by understanding what works well and what needs enhancement. These cookies don't collect information that identifies visitors personally. Data is aggregated and anonymous. We use tools like Google Analytics with anonymized IP addresses. Performance cookies help us optimize page loading times and navigation structures. You can opt-out of these cookies without significantly affecting your experience.

5. Functionality Cookies

Functionality cookies enable enhanced features by remembering your preferences and choices. These may include cookies that remember your language or region selection. They personalize your experience by greeting you with your name if you've created an account. Some functionality cookies remember your text size or contrast settings for accessibility. These cookies may also enable live chat features on our site. Information collected is anonymized and cannot track browsing activity on other websites. Disabling these cookies may result in reduced functionality but won't prevent access to our services.

6. Targeting/Advertising Cookies

Advertising cookies track browsing habits to make advertising more relevant to you and your interests. These cookies remember that you've visited our website and may share this information with advertising partners. They are used to deliver targeted advertising both on our site and through other websites. You may see ads for our services when visiting other sites due to these cookies. We limit tracking through advertising cookies and provide opt-out options. Some advertising partners belong to self-regulation programs that offer preference management tools.

7. First-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are set by our website domain and mostly support core functionality. These cookies are generally safer than third-party cookies as they can't be read by other websites. We use first-party cookies for session management, authentication, and security. Some analytics tools we use operate with first-party cookies for better data accuracy. First-party cookies typically have shorter lifespans than third-party cookies. These cookies may store your preferences for our specific site but don't track you across the web.

8. Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies are set by domains other than bloomfigo.com, primarily by our partners. These include analytics services, social media platforms, and advertising networks. Social media cookies enable sharing capabilities and personalization across platforms. Some third-party services we embed (like maps or videos) may set their own cookies. We carefully select third-party providers with strong privacy commitments. You can manage third-party cookie preferences through our cookie consent tool or browser settings. We're working to minimize reliance on non-essential third-party cookies.

9. Cookie Duration

Session cookies expire when you close your browser and are used for temporary functions. Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period or until manually deleted. Our analytical cookies typically persist for 6-12 months to track trends over time. Preference cookies may last up to 2 years to remember your settings between visits. Advertising cookies often have the longest durations, sometimes several years. We review cookie lifespans regularly to balance functionality with privacy considerations. Essential cookies generally have shorter lifespans than optional cookies.

10. Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings, allowing you to accept, block, or delete them. Most browsers provide options to manage cookie preferences on a site-by-site basis. Mobile devices may have additional settings for controlling trackers. Blocking cookies may result in reduced functionality on our website. Our cookie consent tool lets you opt-in or out of non-essential cookie categories. Be aware that deleting cookies may clear saved preferences and require re-authentication. Browsers occasionally update their cookie management interfaces so settings may change.

11. EU Cookie Law Compliance

We comply with the EU Cookie Law (ePrivacy Directive) and GDPR requirements. Visitors from the EU see a cookie consent banner when first accessing our site. The banner clearly explains our cookie usage and provides granular opt-in options. Consent preferences are stored and respected for return visits. We maintain records of consent in compliance with accountability requirements. EU users can withdraw consent at any time through our preference center. Our systems are regularly audited to ensure continued compliance.

12. California Consumer Privacy Act

For California residents, the CCPA provides specific rights regarding personal information including cookie data. You have the right to know what personal information is collected through cookies. Our cookie policy disclosure serves as this "notice at collection" for CCPA purposes. You may opt-out of the "sale" of personal information through our cookie preference tool. We don't discriminate against users who exercise their privacy rights. CCPA requests can be made through our designated webform or toll-free number.

13. Children's Cookie Usage

We don't knowingly use tracking technologies on pages directed to children under 13. Children's accounts have additional privacy protections limiting data collection. Parental consent is required before setting non-essential cookies for child users. We minimize data collection in our children's gardening programs. Some features may be restricted for child accounts to protect privacy. Parents can review and manage their child's cookie preferences through family accounts. We comply with COPPA regulations regarding children's online privacy.

14. Cookie Security

We implement security measures to protect cookies from unauthorized access. Secure cookies are transmitted only over HTTPS encrypted connections. HttpOnly cookies protect against certain types of cross-site scripting attacks. SameSite cookie attributes prevent some cross-site request forgery attempts. Sensitive cookies are encrypted and signed to prevent tampering. Our security team monitors for unusual cookie-related activity that may indicate attacks. Cookie security is part of our broader application security strategy.

15. Mobile App Cookies

Our mobile applications use similar tracking technologies as our website. Mobile platforms may use equivalent identifiers instead of traditional cookies. App tracking preferences can be managed through device settings. Some app functionality relies on local storage of preferences and settings. We follow platform guidelines for user privacy and data collection. Consistent with our web practices, mobile tracking is minimized while maintaining core functionality. App users receive onboarding screens explaining data practices and controls.

16. Cookie Consent Management

We use a cookie consent management platform to honor user preferences. The initial banner provides clear options for accepting or declining cookies. Visitors can access preference centers to make granular choices about cookie categories. Consent choices are stored and reapplied on future visits. Our system logs consents for compliance documentation purposes. The consent interface is regularly tested for accessibility and usability. Users can change their cookie preferences at any time on our website.

17. Impact of Disabling Cookies

Blocking certain cookies may affect website functionality in various ways. Essential functions like logins and payments may still work, but with reduced convenience. Personalization features may reset to defaults each visit without cookies. Some content or services might become inaccessible without supporting cookies. Analytics that help us improve site performance would be less accurate. Advertising may become less relevant but more repetitive. We aim to degrade gracefully when cookies are restricted to maintain usability.
